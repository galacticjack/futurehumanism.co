Headline Writing FAQ

What makes a good headline?
Good headlines have 6-12 words, use power words and emotional triggers, include numbers when relevant, create curiosity or urgency, and clearly communicate value. The best headlines balance clarity with intrigue.
How long should a headline be?
Optimal headline length is 6-12 words or 50-70 characters. This ensures full visibility in search results and social shares while providing enough context to communicate value.
Do numbers in headlines increase clicks?
Yes, headlines with numbers get 36% more clicks on average. Odd numbers (7, 9, 11) tend to perform slightly better than even numbers. Specific numbers are more credible than rounded ones.
What are power words?
Power words trigger emotional responses that drive action. Examples include: Free, Secret, Proven, Instant, Guaranteed, Exclusive, Simple, Ultimate, and Surprising. Use 1-2 per headline for best results.
